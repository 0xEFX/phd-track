Presentation: The Wheel Turns – Analyzing the Lifecycle of Innovation, Adoption, and Commercialization

Slide 1: Opening – The Old Internet

Title: My Favorite Game and Website

  • Talking Points:
  • "I want to talk to you about my favorite video game. Moonstone for the Amiga."
  • "But actually, I want to tell you about my favorite website: The Moonstone Tavern."
  • Describe the “Old Internet” feel: No ads, cookie popups, notifications, or data-gathering.
  • Emphasize: “It’s what websites used to be—repositories of information, built by the people who cared enough to dedicate their time to them.”
  • Transition: "But today, we’re going to explore what changed, why it changed, and how it fits into the larger cycle of tech."

Slide 2: Introduction to the Cyclical Nature of Tech

Title: Understanding the Cyclical Nature of Technology

  • Talking Points:
  • Introduce the idea of a recurring “tech lifecycle” with phases: innovation, adoption, commercialization, and decline.
  • Discuss the cultural shifts and commercialization impacts within this cycle.
  • Outline the three case studies: IRC, Social Media, and Smartphones.

Slide 3: Overview of the Lifecycle Phases

Title: Phases of the Technology Lifecycle

  • Talking Points:
  • Breakdown of phases: Innovation and Creation, Community Growth, Mass Adoption, Commercialization, Decline and Dissatisfaction.
  • Briefly describe each phase’s relevance across all case studies.

Slide 4: IRC – The Birth of Online Community

Title: IRC: From Niche Platform to Obsolescence

  • Talking Points:
  • Origins of IRC (Internet Relay Chat) as a pioneer for tech communities in the 1980s and 90s.
  • Appeal to tech enthusiasts and the creation of exclusive, tech-centered subcultures.
  • Note its lack of commercialization and how this maintained its niche appeal but limited its longevity.

Slide 5: IRC – Community Growth and Decline

Title: IRC’s Legacy and Cultural Impact

  • Talking Points:
  • How IRC fostered collaborative communities and a DIY ethos.
  • User-generated features and commands that became cultural hallmarks.
  • Explain IRC’s decline as user-friendly, commercialized chat services appeared.

Slide 6: Social Media – MySpace to Facebook to Instagram

Title: Social Media Evolution: MySpace, Facebook, and Instagram

  • Talking Points:
  • MySpace’s rise as an unregulated, creativity-driven social platform.
  • Transition to Facebook: from exclusive, data-driven platform to mainstream adoption.
  • The rise of Instagram: mobile-friendly, photo-centric, and eventually acquired by Facebook.

Slide 7: Social Media – Commercialization and Privacy Trade-offs

Title: From Innovation to Data-Driven Commercialization

  • Talking Points:
  • Facebook and Instagram’s shift from user-centric to ad-centric models.
  • Introduction of algorithms, privacy trade-offs, and impact on user experience.
  • Discuss the resulting changes in user culture and platform design.

Slide 8: Smartphones – From PDAs to the iPhone

Title: Smartphones: From Simple PDAs to Essential Devices

  • Talking Points:
  • Brief history of PDAs (e.g., Palm Pilot) as organizational tools for tech-savvy users.
  • Introduction of iPhone and Android, transforming mobile tech into lifestyle devices.
  • Discuss how smartphones expanded the app ecosystem and became versatile, essential tools.

Slide 9: Smartphones – Data, Surveillance, and Ethics

Title: Smartphones, Data Tracking, and Surveillance Capitalism

  • Talking Points:
  • Apple and Google’s data collection for personalization and profit.
  • “Surveillance capitalism” and its effects on user privacy and autonomy.
  • Ethical implications and the trade-off between innovation and privacy.

Slide 10: Case Study Comparison – Key Takeaways

Title: Comparing IRC, Social Media, and Smartphones

  • Talking Points:
  • Summarize each case study’s role in illustrating the tech lifecycle.
  • IRC: niche without commercialization, sustaining culture but limited growth.
  • Social Media: rapid commercialization with privacy and cultural trade-offs.
  • Smartphones: an example of innovation leading to data-driven surveillance.

Slide 11: Discussion – Implications of the Tech Lifecycle

Title: Implications of the Technology Lifecycle

  • Talking Points:
  • Discuss the challenges commercialization brings to innovation and community.
  • Questions of sustainable, niche communities in commercial ecosystems.
  • Ethical considerations: Can we retain original innovation without sacrificing user privacy?

Slide 12: Conclusion – Breaking the Cycle?

Title: Conclusion: Can We Break the Cycle?

  • Talking Points:
  • Summarize the cyclical nature of tech and its impacts.
  • Introduce “breaking the cycle”: "The key may lie in keeping management consultants as far away from tech as possible!"
  • End with a question: How might we sustain tech’s original ethos in future innovations?

Slide 13: Q&A

Title: Questions and Discussion

  • Talking Points:
  • Invite questions and discussion.
  • Encourage sharing of audience experiences with commercialization and tech cycles.

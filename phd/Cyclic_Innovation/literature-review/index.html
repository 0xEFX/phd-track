Diffusion of Innovations — Everett M. Rogers — 1962 — Book — Explains how new ideas spread through adopter groups over time; gives you the “adoption curve” backbone you can extend into “what happens when commercialization hits.”

Platform Capitalism — Nick Srnicek — 2016 — Book — Argues platforms structurally trend toward data extraction, monopoly, and rent-seeking; perfect for explaining why “profit pressure” isn’t accidental, it’s baked in.

Understanding Media: The Extensions of Man — Marshall McLuhan — 1964 — Book — “The medium is the message” framing: the form of media reshapes society; supports your claim that algorithmic/monetized platform changes transform culture regardless of content.

Amusing Ourselves to Death: Public Discourse in the Age of Show Business — Neil Postman — 1985 — Book — Shows how media optimized for entertainment corrodes public discourse; ports cleanly to feeds/engagement metrics and helps you argue degradation is a predictable media effect.

The Age of Surveillance Capitalism — Shoshana Zuboff — 2019 — Book — Documents how behavioral data becomes the core profit engine and incentives shift toward prediction/control; directly supports your “information becomes extraction” thesis.

Networks of Power: Electrification in Western Society, 1880–1930 — Thomas P. Hughes — 1993 — Book — Classic large-scale sociotechnical history showing tech systems evolve with institutions/culture/power; useful for grounding your cycle as “systems behavior,” not just internet drama.

The Social Construction of Technological Systems — Wiebe E. Bijker, Thomas P. Hughes, Trevor Pinch (eds.) — 1987 — Edited book — Foundational STS/SCOT collection arguing technologies are shaped by social groups and politics; gives you academic cover for “platform outcomes are socially produced.”

We Have Never Been Modern — Bruno Latour — 1991 (English translation 1993) — Book — Core STS argument that “social” and “technical” are inseparable networks; helps you frame platforms as hybrid systems whose “decay” is socio-economic, not purely technical.

It’s Complicated: The Social Lives of Networked Teens — danah boyd — 2014 — Book — Empirical, sociologically rigorous analysis of how social platforms change behavior as they scale; excellent for grounding “community degradation” in observed user dynamics rather than nostalgia.

The Filter Bubble: What the Internet Is Hiding from You — Eli Pariser — 2011 — Book — Early, influential account of algorithmic personalization fragmenting shared reality; supports your claim that commercialization reshapes information quality, not just delivery.

The Culture of Connectivity — José van Dijck — 2013 — Book — Analyzes social media platforms as socio-economic systems, not neutral tools; bridges your cultural argument with platform political economy.

YouTube: Online Video and Participatory Culture — Jean Burgess & Joshua Green — 2009 — Book — Strong historical account of YouTube’s transition from participatory culture to commercial platform; anchors your YouTube case study in peer-reviewed scholarship.

Custodians of the Internet — Tarleton Gillespie — 2018 — Book — Definitive work on moderation, governance, and platform power; excellent for explaining how degradation is enforced and normalized at scale.

Understanding Media — Marshall McLuhan — 1964 — Book — You already implicitly rely on this; your file correctly treats McLuhan as a conceptual ancestor rather than a novelty citation.

Platform Capitalism — Nick Srnicek — 2016 — Book — Already core to your argument; your usage is correct and disciplined, not cargo-culted.

The Age of Surveillance Capitalism — Shoshana Zuboff — 2019 — Book — You deploy this appropriately in the smartphone and social media sections; it cleanly supports your causal mechanism.
